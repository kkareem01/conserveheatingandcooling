When You Need It

Signs your ducts need cleaning

1

Visible dust around vents

If you see dust streaks above or below the registers, that's coming from inside the duct, not from outside.

2

After renovation

Drywall dust and sawdust get pulled into the system during construction. Always clean after a remodel.

3

New home purchase

You don't know when the previous owner last cleaned them — or what got into them. Start fresh.

Duct Cleaning Questions

Duct cleaning FAQ

How often should I clean my air ducts?

Every 3–5 years for most homes. More often if you have pets, smokers, allergies, or just renovated. Less often if the home is new and tightly sealed.

How much does duct cleaning cost?

Priced by # of vents/returns and home size. A typical 3-bedroom home with 10–14 vents runs a flat upfront rate — no per-vent upcharges on the day of service. We give you the number when you book.

Watch out for the $99 duct cleaning ads?

Yes. Those usually means "blow air through one vent" — not a real cleaning. The $99 turns into $800+ once they show you "all the mold" they found. Get a real quote from a licensed contractor.

How long does it take?

A typical home takes 3–5 hours done right. Anything significantly faster means corners got cut.

What's the difference between duct cleaning and vent cleaning?

Duct cleaning is the full system — trunk lines, branch runs, plenum, blower. Vent cleaning is just the supply/return registers and a few feet inside. We do both.
