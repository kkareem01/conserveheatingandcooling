What We Fix

Common mini-split issues we solve

Mini-splits have their own diagnostic playbook. We've been working on them for years.

Error codes & lockouts

P4, E6, F1 — every manufacturer codes them differently. We read them, decode them, and trace to the actual root cause.

Refrigerant leaks

Low refrigerant means weak cooling and a struggling compressor. We find the leak, fix it, and recharge to spec — not just top it off.

Indoor head water leaks

Drip pan, condensate line, or clogged pump. We clear it, flush it, and pitch the drain right so it doesn't come back next month.

Outdoor unit not running

Capacitor, fan motor, contactor, or PCB. We test each in sequence to find the actual failed part — not throw new ones in until something works.

Weak airflow or smell

Filter, blower wheel, and coil cleaning. Mini-splits build up biofilm fast — and you smell it before you see the airflow drop.

Remote / Wi-Fi not connecting

Receiver fault, dead remote, or Wi-Fi module bricked? We troubleshoot the controls path and reset the system if it's been frozen.

Mini-Split Repair Questions

Mini-split repair FAQ

My mini-split is flashing an error code — what does it mean?

Error codes vary by manufacturer — Mitsubishi, Daikin, LG, and Fujitsu each have their own list. They point to a sensor, communication, or component fault. Send us the code when you book and we'll usually know what parts to bring.

How much does a mini-split repair cost?

Flat diagnostic fee gets you a written repair quote. Most common fixes — capacitor, sensor, drain — are inexpensive. Compressor or PCB replacements are bigger jobs; we'll walk you through repair vs. replace economics when relevant.

Can you work on my brand?

Yes — Mitsubishi, Daikin, LG, Fujitsu, Pioneer, Senville, MrCool, and others. If it's been installed in Gwinnett County, we've probably worked on it.

My indoor head is dripping water — emergency?

Shut it off and call. It's usually a clogged condensate drain — easy fix — but water dripping through drywall is the real damage. Same-day slot if you call before noon.

When is it time to replace instead of repair?

Compressor failure on a 12+ year unit, or a PCB on an out-of-warranty system where parts are scarce. We tell you the honest economics — most mini-splits are worth repairing for 10+ years if maintained.
